Use this service to submit a concern or complaint about the safety of a high-rise building, if:

  • you live in the high-rise building, or
  • you're acting on behalf of someone who lives in the high-rise building, or
  • your job requires spending time in the high-rise building

A high-rise building is:

  • either 18 metres high or has 7 or more storeys
    • AND
  • contains 2 or more residential units

Before using this service you must first raise the concern or complaint with your building manager (if you own the property) or your landlord (if you rent the property) and give them reasonable time to respond.

You can choose to remain anonymous to your building manager (and all building staff) within this process.

Submitting a concern or complaint takes around 5 minutes.

When not to use this service

You should not use this service if your concern or complaint is about:

  • noise complaints (contact your local authority)
  • neighbour disputes (see GOV.UK's guidance)
  • environmental issues (contact your local authority)
  • criminal damage or vandalism (call the non-emergency police line on 150)
  • violent crime or theft (call the non-emergency police line on 150)

What happens when you raise a complaint

Within 48 hours of receiving your complaint the Building Safety Regulator will decide:

  • if your complaint has come to the right place
  • if there are other complaints about the same issue
  • if any more information or evidence is needed
  • the severity level of your complaint

The regulator will contact you about their decision, and tell you what their next actions will be if your complaint is upheld.

For severe complaints, the Regulator will inspect the building, write a report, and work with the accountable person for the building to resolve the issue. The Regulator can enforce remedies if there has been a breach of law.

For less severe complaints, the Regulator will contact the accountable person for the building and recommend actions to resolve the issue.

If you have asked to be anonymous, the Regulator will not reveal your identity to the accountable person.

Before you start

You'll be asked for details about who you've already raised this concern to and when. You'll also be asked to provide any supporting documentation (such as photographs of the issue or any relevant email exchanges).
