Use this service to submit a concern or complaint about the safety of a high-rise building, if:

  • you live in the high-rise building OR
  • you're acting on behalf of someone who lives in the high-rise building OR
  • your job requires spending time in the high-rise building

A high-rise building has at least 7 storeys, or is 18m high and either has at least 2 residential units or is a hospital or care home (during design and construction).

Before using this service you must first raise the concern or complaint with your building manager (if you own the property) or your landlord (if you rent the property) and give them reasonable time to respond.

You can choose to remain anonymous to your building manager (and all building staff) within this process.

Submitting a concern or complaint takes around 5 minutes.

Before you start

You'll be asked for details about who you've already raised this concern to and when. You'll also be asked to provide any supporting documentation. You'll be contacted by the Building Safety Regulator if more evidence is needed.

When not to use this service

You should not use this service if your concern or complaint is about:

  • noise complaints (contact your local authority)
  • neighbour disputes (see GOV.UK's guidance)
  • environmental issues (contact your local authority)
  • criminal damage or vandalism (call the non-emergency police line on 150)
  • violent crime or theft (call the non-emergency police line on 150)

If you need help using this service

The quickest way to raise a concern or complaint with us is using this online service. If you can't use this service, though, you can phone our support team and we will fill in the form with you.
