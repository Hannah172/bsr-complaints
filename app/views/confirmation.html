What happens next

Thank you for raising your complaint with us. We take all complaints seriously.

After your complaint is submitted, it will be read by the Building Safety Regulator’s triage team (a team who review building safety complaints). They will decide:

  • if this complaint has come to the right place
  • if there have been other similar complaints already made
  • if any more information or evidence is needed
  • the severity level of the complaint (red or amber)

Once this has been decided (within 48 hours of receiving the complaint) you will be contacted with the next steps.

For amber level complaints, the triage team will contact you and the accountable person for the building to resolve the issue remotely.

For red level complaints, there will be a complaints inspector assigned who will deal directly with the building’s accountable person, arranging an inspection, writing up a report, and – if the complaint is upheld – working with the accountable person to resolve the issue. If there has been a breach of law, this may lead to enforcement (the accountable person will be forced to solve the issue).

If you have asked to be anonymous, the BSR will not reveal your identity to the accountable person and all correspondence will be separate.
